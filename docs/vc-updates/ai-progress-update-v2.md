# CRWD AI: Where We're At

**We didn't build a chatbot. We built a team.**

## The short version

CRWD now runs on a squad of AI specialists, not one generic assistant. Every
member conversation gets routed to the agent built for that exact job —
finding a gig, getting paid, fixing something broken — and each one knows
CRWD's real rules and real data cold. No scripts, no guesswork.

That's the core idea, in plain terms: instead of one AI trying to be good at
everything, we split the work the way a well-run support team would — a
specialist for each type of problem, each one narrow and very good at its
one job, working together behind a single conversation. When a member
messages us, the system reads the situation first, then hands off to
whichever specialist actually owns that problem. That's what makes it
**agentic** rather than a chatbot: it's a team making a decision about who
should answer, not one script trying to cover every case.

## The lineup

- **Gig Discovery** — finds the right gig, and the right store to do it at
  (address, phone, hours — pulled live).
- **Gig Execution** — gets a member from "approved" to "proof submitted"
  without a rejected submission.
- **Receipt & Proof Validator** — checks submitted proof and is being built
  out to flag fraud before it reaches payout.
- **Payment Status** — real answers on real payouts, not "check your email."
- **App Navigator** — never lets a member get lost in the app.
- **Troubleshooting** — fixes the common stuff before it becomes a ticket.
- **Reminders & Follow-ups** — chases deadlines and surfaces the next gig
  before a member has to ask.
- **Conversation Labeling & Reporting** — tags every conversation so we
  always know what members actually need.
- **Escalation** — knows exactly when to get a human, fast.

Underneath it all: live internet search, so the agents can answer
real-world questions — store hours, phone numbers, product details — on the
spot instead of guessing.

## It remembers, too

Alongside the agents, there's a memory layer that tracks each member as a
person, not just as a message. It's not a transcript we can search back
through — it's a running, evolving profile of who that member is, built up
across every conversation they've ever had with us: their gig history, what
they've asked before, where they got stuck, how they like to be helped.

The result is simple but powerful: the system never starts cold. It doesn't
re-ask what it already knows, and it tailors how it helps based on who's
actually on the other end of the conversation — a brand-new member gets a
different conversation than someone who's done fifty gigs. The agents decide
*what* to help with; this memory layer decides *how* to tailor it to that
specific person. No repeated questions, no cold starts — the 10th
conversation is better than the 1st, and it keeps compounding from there.

## How we built it — and how we keep building it

This wasn't guesswork. We mined **over 400,000 real member messages** to
find the patterns that actually matter, and that's the process, not a
one-time step. We ship, watch what real conversations reveal, and fold it
straight back into the agents — the lineup above is a snapshot of a system
that's iterating constantly, not a finished product we're maintaining.

## What it's already doing for us

- **Instant coverage** — every member gets a specialist, immediately, at any
  hour, at any volume — no hiring, no ramp time.
- **Fewer dead-end conversations** — members get routed to the right answer
  instead of bouncing around, and the ones that need a human get there fast,
  not after three wrong turns.
- **A support team that gets cheaper to run as it scales** — the more
  conversations it sees, the less each one costs to handle well.
- **Data that compounds** — every conversation makes the system, and our
  understanding of our members, sharper. That's not true of a static FAQ bot,
  and it's not something a competitor can shortcut by copying a script.

## What's next

This is v1. The same pipeline that produced this lineup keeps running — more
specialists, deeper memory, and more of the support flow handled end-to-end
without a human in the loop.
